### How Reels Differs from TikTok

Instagram Reels prioritizes **shareability and saves** over pure watch time. The algorithm favors content that:

1. Gets **sent via DM** (strongest signal)

## üì§ Shareability Engineering

### The "Send This To" Factor

Instagram's algorithm LOVES content that gets DM shared. Design for:

#### Direct Prompts
```
"Send this to someone who needs to hear it"
"Tag a friend who does this"
"DM this to your [type of person]"
```

#### Implicit Shareability
- Relatable scenarios ("This is SO my friend Sarah")
- Useful information (recipes, tips, lists)
- Inside jokes for specific communities
- "OMG I had to share this" moments

### Save-Worthy Content Structure

Content people save falls into:
1. **Reference content** - Lists, tutorials, recipes
2. **Inspiration** - Aesthetic, motivational
3. **Information** - Facts, tips, how-tos
4. **Entertainment** - Want to rewatch/share later

## üìä Content Lifecycle

### The First 30 Minutes

Even more critical than TikTok:
- Initial push to followers + small test batch (~100-500)
- Engagement in first 30 min determines reach ceiling
- Shares/saves weighted heavily in this window

### Reels Distribution Phases

```
Phase 1 (0-1h):    Followers + test batch
Phase 2 (1-6h):    Explore candidates emerge
Phase 3 (6-48h):   Explore/viral expansion
Phase 4 (48h+):    Continued reach or plateau
```

### Posting Time

Best times for Reels:
- 11 AM - 1 PM (lunch scrolling)
- 7 PM - 9 PM (evening engagement)
- Weekends generally higher engagement

## üö´ Shadowban & Reach Limits

### Actions That Limit Reach

- Watermarks from other platforms (TikTok logo)
- Recycled content without modification
- Excessive hashtag spam
- Violating community guidelines
- Low-resolution uploads
- Controversial political content

### Recovery Strategies

1. Remove any watermarked content
2. Create original content for 2 weeks
3. Engage authentically on platform
4. Ensure high-quality uploads
